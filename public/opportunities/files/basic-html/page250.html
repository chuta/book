                                    SEIZING OPPORTUNITIES IN THE DIGITAL ASSET ECONOMY |The approved products, led by BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT), Fidelity's WiseOrigin Bitcoin Fund (FBTC), and products from Invesco, VanEck, Franklin Templeton, andothers, each hold actual Bitcoin in custody through regulated custodians, priced dailybased on standardised reference rates, and trade on major US exchanges like NYSE Arcaand CBOE. They charge management fees ranging from fifteen to thirty basis pointsannually, compete directly on fee and fund size, and are accessible through any standardbrokerage account. The friction involved in buying exposure to Bitcoin through an ETF isnow indistinguishable from the friction of buying any other ETF, which was precisely thepoint.The inflow dynamics revealed immediately how large the pent-up demand was.BlackRock's IBIT reached ten billion dollars in assets under management faster than anyETF in history, in approximately seven weeks, a milestone that took gold ETFs two years toachieve. By early 2026, the aggregate assets under management in US spot Bitcoin ETFshad exceeded sixty billion dollars, representing approximately four percent of Bitcoin'stotal supply and establishing these products as among the most significant new assetmanagement vehicles launched in a generation.Ethereum followed. The SEC approved spot Ethereum ETFs in May 2024, enablinginstitutional access to the second-largest digital asset through the same regulated ETFwrapper.
