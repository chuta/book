                                    SEIZING OPPORTUNITIES IN THE DIGITAL ASSET ECONOMY |CHAPTER 3The Cryptoeconomic Stack: Protocols, Tokens& Incentive DesignIn traditional finance, the rules of the game are written by the institutions thatbenefit most from them. In cryptoeconomics, the rules are written in code, andanyone can read them before they decide to play.%u2014 Chimezie ChutaEvery economy has a stack. The traditional financial economy runs on a stack ofinfrastructure, physical currency at the base, commercial banks above it, paymentnetworks above those, and consumer-facing financial products at the top. Each layerdepends on the layers beneath it, each layer extracts value from the layers it serves, and therules governing the interactions between layers are set by a combination of regulation,contract law, and the negotiating power of large institutions.The cryptoeconomic stack follows the same basic logic, layers of infrastructure, eachbuilding on those beneath it, but differs in one foundational respect. The rules governinginteractions between layers are not set by institutions or regulators. They are set by code,encoded in protocols, and enforced by the cryptographic and economic incentives that theprevious chapter described. Anyone can read them. Anyone can build on them. And theycannot be changed by any single party without the agreement of the network.This chapter is about how that stack is organised, how value flows through it, and how thetokens that power each layer are designed to align the interests of participants with thehealth of the system as a whole. Understanding the stack is not an academic exercise. It isthe prerequisite for evaluating any investment, any protocol, and any business opportunityin the digital asset ecosystem, because everything in that ecosystem is built somewhere onthis stack, and its position on the stack determines its risk profile, its competitivedynamics, and its long-term value capture potential.%u2014 31 %u2014
