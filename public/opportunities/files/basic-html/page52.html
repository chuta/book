In the Nigerian context, and more broadly acrossmarkets with volatile currencies, capital controls, or unreliable banking infrastructure, theframe is different. Self-custody is not merely a preference. For many participants, it is aneconomic necessity.The naira depreciated by more than fifty percent against the dollar in 2023, erasing thepurchasing power of savings held in naira-denominated bank accounts regardless of theinterest rate earned. Capital controls imposed by the Central Bank of Nigeria have, atvarious points, restricted the ability of Nigerians to access foreign currencies throughformal banking channels. The banking system's history of freezing accounts associatedwith cryptocurrency transactions, most significantly through the 2021 CBN directive,created a direct incentive for cryptocurrency users to hold their assets outside of anysystem that could be reached by a regulatory order.In this context, the ability to hold dollar-denominated stablecoins in a self-custodiedwallet, assets that cannot be devalued by monetary policy, cannot be frozen by a bank, andcannot be seized without physical access to the device on which the private keys are stored,is a genuinely meaningful form of financial sovereignty. It is not perfect: hardware walletscan be confiscated, seed phrases can be coerced, and the volatility of most non-stablecoindigital assets introduces a different form of risk. But for a participant who has correctlyunderstood the tools available to them and applied the security practices described in thischapter, self-custody of digital assets represents a level of financial autonomy that was notavailable at any price before these technologies existed.This is the deepest argument for taking self-custody seriously: not that it is technicallyinteresting, not that it is philosophically aligned with cypherpunk ideals, but that it is apractical tool for financial resilience in environments where the traditional financialsystem cannot be relied upon to protect its participants. Africa has more of thoseenvironments than any other continent. And the people who understand how to navigatethem, who have internalised the security practices, the key management disciplines, andthe threat awareness that self-custody requires, are the ones who will benefit most fromthe transition that is already underway.%u2014 52 %u2014
