                                    SEIZING OPPORTUNITIES IN THE DIGITAL ASSET ECONOMY |Setting Up Self-Custody: The Practical FrameworkUnderstanding the theory of self-custody is the beginning. Building the practice is whatactually protects your assets. The following framework is designed for participants whoare serious about taking custody of meaningful amounts of digital assets.The first principle is separation of concerns. Your seed phrase should never exist in digitalform, not in a text file on your computer, not in a cloud storage service, not in an email toyourself, not in a photograph stored on your phone. The seed phrase is an analogue secretin a digital world, and the moment it touches the internet, even briefly, it is potentiallycompromised. Write it on paper. Better yet, stamp it into a metal plate, stainless steel ortitanium, that will survive fire, water, and physical impact. Store the physical backup in alocation that is secure against theft, accessible to you or a designated trusted person in anemergency, and separated from the device it backs up.The second principle is tiered custody. Not all of your assets require the same level ofsecurity, and applying maximum security to everything creates friction that most peoplewill eventually route around. A practical tiered structure has three levels: a hot wallet witha small amount, the digital equivalent of the cash in your physical wallet, for everydaytransactions and DeFi interactions; a hardware wallet for medium-term holdings that youdo not need to access frequently; and deep cold storage, ideally a multisig arrangement, forlong-term holdings that you do not need to access at all under ordinary circumstances.The third principle is redundancy without exposure. A single hardware wallet with a singlecopy of the seed phrase stored in a single location is vulnerable to physical loss ordestruction. The seed phrase should be backed up in at least two geographically separatelocations. The hardware wallet itself should ideally be backed up with a second deviceinitialised from the same seed phrase. But every additional copy of the seed phrase is anadditional attack surface, each copy must be as securely stored as the original.The fourth principle is tested recovery. Most people set up a hardware wallet, write downtheir seed phrase, and never verify that the recovery process actually works. Before youstore any meaningful amount of assets on a self-custody wallet, test the recovery process:wipe the device and restore it from the seed phrase. Discover any problems before theymatter rather than when they matter.The fifth principle is inheritance planning.
