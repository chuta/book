The work is ongoing,technically feasible, and not yet urgent, but it is the kind of long-lead infrastructure preparationthat responsible protocol stewardship requires.The Internet of Value: Machine Economies and AutonomousFinanceThe internet was described in its early years as an information superhighway, a metaphorthat turned out to be both accurate and insufficient. The internet moved information, but italso moved commerce, culture, politics, and social connection in ways that the 'informationsuperhighway' metaphor failed to anticipate. We are in a similar moment with what isbeing called the 'internet of value', the emerging infrastructure for moving value acrossnetworks with the same speed, programmability, and global reach that the internet gave toinformation.The internet of value is not just about people sending money faster. It is about the ability toembed value transfer into any connected device, any digital interaction, any automatedprocess. A supply chain where each transfer of custody triggers an automatic payment. Apower grid where solar energy producers receive micropayments for each kilowatt-hourfed into the grid. A content platform where creators receive direct, immediate paymenteach time their work is accessed, without intermediaries taking a percentage. Anautonomous vehicle that pays for its own charging, parking, and road use without a humanowner managing those transactions. These are not science fiction; they are applications ofexisting blockchain infrastructure to existing use cases, and they are being built.The machine economy dimension of the internet of value is particularly significant. As AIagents become capable of acting autonomously in digital environments, executing tasks,accessing services, and making decisions on behalf of human principals, they require theability to transact autonomously. An AI agent that can browse the internet, generatecontent, and analyse data, but cannot pay for the services it uses, is limited in ways thatblockchain-native payment systems address.
