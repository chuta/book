Curve Finance, the dominant AMM for stablecoin trading, usesa different mathematical formula optimised for assets that should trade at near-identicalprices, achieving dramatically lower slippage for stablecoin swaps than Uniswap's constantproduct formula.Key Concept: How AMM Pricing Works (x %u00d7 y = k)A Uniswap pool holds 100 ETH and 200,000 USDC. The constant k = 100 x 200,000 =20,000,000. A trader wants to buy 10 ETH. After the trade, the pool holds 90 ETH. To keep kconstant: 90 x new_USDC = 20,000,000, so new_USDC = 222,222. The trader paid 22,222 USDCfor 10 ETH, an effective price of $2,222 per ETH, versus the pre-trade ratio of $2,000. The extra$222 is price impact (slippage) caused by moving the pool's ratio. In deep pools with manyliquidity providers, this slippage is minimal. In shallow pools, it can be severe.Key Concept: Impermanent LossImpermanent loss occurs when you provide liquidity to an AMM and the price ratio of the twotokens changes. If you had simply held the tokens instead of depositing them, you would havemore value. The loss is 'impermanent' because it disappears if prices return to the original ratio,but if you withdraw while the ratio has shifted, the loss becomes permanent. Rule of thumb: thegreater the price divergence between the two tokens, the greater the impermanent loss. Forstable pairs (USDC/USDT), impermanent loss is near zero. For volatile pairs (ETH/small-captoken), it can exceed all fee income.Decentralised Lending: Borrowing Without a BankThe second major pillar of DeFi is decentralised lending, protocols that allow participantsto lend their assets and earn interest, or borrow assets by providing collateral, without anyinstitution making credit decisions or holding custody of funds.Aave and Compound are the two most important lending protocols. Their basic mechanismis similar: lenders deposit assets into a pool and receive interest-bearing tokensrepresenting their claim on the pool.
