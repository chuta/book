                                    SEIZING OPPORTUNITIES IN THE DIGITAL ASSET ECONOMY |CHAPTER 15Investing in Digital Assets: A Framework for theNew EraRisk taxonomy, portfolio construction, on-chain due diligence, and avoiding common ruinThe digital asset market will make you rich, it will make you poor, and it will doboth to many of the same people. The difference between the two outcomes isalmost never luck. It is almost always preparation.%u2014 Chimezie ChutaThere is a peculiar quality to investment advice about digital assets: most of it is written bypeople who got lucky and are now explaining, with great confidence, why their luck waswisdom. The markets of 2017, 2020, and 2021 rewarded almost any purchase of almostany digital asset held for twelve months. In those environments, the bar for being asuccessful investor was so low that sophisticated analysis was not merely unnecessary; itwas actively misleading, because it produced conclusions that underperformed the naivestrategy of buying everything.But markets do not remain permanently in phases where everything goes up. The 2018bear market wiped out ninety percent of peak valuations across the asset class. The 2022bear market destroyed not just speculative projects but some of the most heavily marketedand institutionally-backed names in the ecosystem, Luna, FTX, Celsius, Three ArrowsCapital, Voyager. The participants who were ruined in 2022 were not primarily naive retailinvestors entering the market for the first time. Many were experienced participants whohad survived 2018, accumulated significant capital through the 2020 to 2021 bull market,and then made specific, identifiable errors in how they managed that capital.This chapter is not about picking winners. Picking winners in digital asset markets,identifying in advance which specific tokens will appreciate and which will not, is asdifficult as picking winners in early-stage equity markets, and the informationasymmetries, manipulation, and structural factors that affect price in digital asset marketsmake it, in some respects, harder. This chapter is about something more durable: aframework for thinking about digital asset investment that reduces the probability of ruin,%u2014 183 %u2014
