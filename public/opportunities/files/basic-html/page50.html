                                    SEIZING OPPORTUNITIES IN THE DIGITAL ASSET ECONOMY |Malware is the second major threat vector. Clipboard-hijacking malware that replacescopied wallet addresses with attacker-controlled addresses has been responsible forsignificant losses, users who copied an address to paste into a transaction field discovered,too late, that the address they pasted was not the one they copied. Keyloggers that captureseed phrases entered into software wallets. Screen-capture malware that records seedphrases displayed during wallet setup. The primary defence is device hygiene: keepingoperating systems and software updated, using dedicated devices for high-valuetransactions where feasible, and never entering a seed phrase on any device that has beenused for general internet browsing, email, or software downloads from untrusted sources.Smart contract risk is the threat vector that is specific to DeFi participants. When youinteract with a DeFi protocol, you typically need to grant that protocol permission to moveyour tokens, a transaction called an approval. Malicious protocols, or legitimate protocolswith malicious contract upgrades, can use unlimited approvals to drain wallets of allapproved tokens at any time. The defence involves regularly auditing and revokingunnecessary approvals using tools like Revoke.cash or the approval management featuresbuilt into hardware wallets, and never granting unlimited approvals unless you have aspecific and current need for them.Social engineering, the manipulation of people rather than systems, is the most difficultthreat to defend against technically, because it targets human psychology rather thansoftware vulnerabilities. Fake 'support' accounts on social media that offer to help withwallet problems. Impersonators of project founders who offer private investmentopportunities. Romantic relationships cultivated over months before a request to invest ina 'platform' is made. These attacks succeed not because their victims are unintelligent butbecause they are designed by sophisticated operators to exploit specific cognitivevulnerabilities, urgency, authority, reciprocity, and the desire to believe in good fortune.The primary defence is scepticism maintained as a discipline, not as an occasionalprecaution.Case Study: FTX %u2014 The Cost of Custodial TrustAt its peak in 2021, FTX was valued at $32 billion and its founder, Sam Bankman-Fried, wascelebrated as one of the most trustworthy figures in the industry, a vocal advocate for regulationand responsible crypto management. In November 2022, it emerged that FTX had been secretlylending customer funds to its affiliated trading firm, Alameda Research, which had sufferedcatastrophic losses.
