                                    SEIZING OPPORTUNITIES IN THE DIGITAL ASSET ECONOMY |CHAPTER 20Institutional Entry Points: ETFs, Custody &On-Chain FinanceHow the world's largest pools of capital entered digital assets, what infrastructure they demanded,and what it means for every participant in the ecosystemWhen BlackRock files for a Bitcoin ETF, it is not making a statement abouttechnology. It is making a statement about demand. Institutional capital doesnot lead markets. It follows them, slowly, carefully, with extensive legalpreparation, and when it arrives, it changes the landscape permanently. Itarrived in 2024. The landscape has not finished changing.%u2014 Chimezie ChutaThere is a date that deserves to be understood as a turning point in the history of digitalassets: January 10, 2024. On that date, the United States Securities and ExchangeCommission approved eleven spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds simultaneously, ending adecade-long regulatory standoff that had blocked institutional capital from enteringBitcoin through the regulated investment vehicles that large allocators require. Withineleven days, those funds had accumulated ten billion dollars in net inflows %u2014 the fastestgrowth of any ETF category in the history of the American financial industry.The significance of that approval was not primarily about price. Bitcoin's priceperformance in 2024 and 2025 reflected many factors, and attributing it solely to ETFapproval oversimplifies a complex market dynamic. The significance was structural: itcreated a regulated, custody-safe, compliance-audited pathway for pension funds,endowments, sovereign wealth funds, registered investment advisers, and family offices toallocate to Bitcoin without holding the asset directly, without managing private keys, andwithout the regulatory ambiguity that had kept most institutional allocators on thesidelines.That structural change, the creation of regulated entry points for institutional capital, iswhat this chapter is about. It begins with ETFs because they represent the most accessibleand most scrutinised institutional entry point. It extends to custody infrastructure, becausethe custody question was the decisive practical barrier for most institutional allocatorsbefore regulated ETFs existed.
