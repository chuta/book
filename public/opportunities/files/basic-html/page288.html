This is not a knock on African markets specifically; it applies equally toretail electricity users in the US, UK, and Europe.Altcoin Mining: The Long Tail of Proof-of-WorkBitcoin is not the only proof-of-work cryptocurrency, and GPU mining, while economicallyirrational for Bitcoin, remains viable for certain altcoin networks that usememory-intensive hashing algorithms designed to resist ASIC optimisation. EthereumClassic, Kaspa, Ravencoin, and various other networks use algorithms that retainmeaningful GPU competitiveness, creating a GPU mining ecosystem that operates inparallel with Bitcoin's ASIC-dominated mining industry.GPU mining economics are more accessible at the individual level than ASIC Bitcoinmining: a gaming-grade graphics card can participate in altcoin mining without requiringindustrial-scale capital. The economics, however, are highly volatile. Altcoin miningprofitability is a function of the coin's price, the network difficulty, and the electricity cost,the same variables as Bitcoin mining, but with significantly higher price volatility for mostaltcoins and much thinner liquidity that makes converting mining rewards to stable valuemore complex.The 'merge mining' concept, where miners can simultaneously mine two networks thatshare the same hashing algorithm without additional computational cost, creates someefficiency improvements for miners in compatible networks.
