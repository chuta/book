                                    SEIZING OPPORTUNITIES IN THE DIGITAL ASSET ECONOMY |The Technologies of the Ownership InternetWeb3 is not a single technology. It is an ecosystem of complementary technologies, eachaddressing a different aspect of the ownership problem. Understanding what thesetechnologies are and how they fit together is essential for evaluating which Web3applications represent genuine innovation and which are window dressing on centralisedsystems.Blockchain and smart contracts are the foundational layer, covered in detail in thepreceding chapters. They provide the trustless settlement infrastructure and theprogrammable logic that makes decentralised ownership technically possible. Without apublic blockchain, there is no verifiable source of truth for who owns what. Without smartcontracts, there is no way to enforce the rules of ownership programmatically without atrusted intermediary.Decentralised storage addresses a problem that pure blockchain architectures cannot fullysolve: the cost of storing large amounts of data on a blockchain is prohibitive. NFTs, forexample, typically store the ownership record on-chain but store the underlying asset, theimage, the music file, the video, off-chain, on decentralised storage networks like IPFS (theInterPlanetary File System) or Arweave. These networks distribute data across manynodes, making it resistant to censorship and single points of failure, without requiringevery participant to store the entire dataset. The reliability of NFT ownership is thereforedependent not just on the blockchain record but on the persistence of the off-chain storagepointing to the actual asset, a dependency that has occasionally exposed the fragility ofpoorly designed NFT projects.Decentralised identity and naming systems give users persistent, self-sovereign identitiesthat are not dependent on any platform. The Ethereum Name Service, or ENS, allows usersto register human-readable names, alice.eth, that resolve to blockchain addresses, much asDNS resolves domain names to IP addresses. Critically, ENS names are owned by theirregistrants as NFTs and cannot be seized or transferred by any central authority.Decentralised identity protocols like those being developed by the W3C allow users tocreate verifiable credentials, proof of membership, proof of qualification, proof of identity,that are controlled by the user rather than stored in a platform's database.Decentralised autonomous organisations, or DAOs, are the governance layer of theownership internet.
