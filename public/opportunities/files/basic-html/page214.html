                                    SEIZING OPPORTUNITIES IN THE DIGITAL ASSET ECONOMY |The DAI Savings Rate, MakerDAO's mechanism for setting the yield on DAI deposited in theprotocol's savings module, has been used as a monetary policy tool to manage DAI supplyand demand, at times offering yields that match or exceed those on centralised platforms.Holders who want to maintain exposure to a decentralised stablecoin rather than USDC orUSDT can earn competitive rates while keeping their holdings in an asset that cannot befrozen by a centralised issuer.Higher stablecoin yields above ten to twelve percent are achievable but require explicitlytaking on additional risk. Newer platforms offer higher rates to attract deposits, but anewer platform has a shorter track record, less battle-tested code, and potentially lessexperienced risk management. Exotic collateral types within a lending protocol cangenerate higher yields but expose lenders to losses if the collateral performs poorly duringa market shock. The principle is consistent throughout: higher yield means you areaccepting higher risk, and your job is to evaluate whether that risk is appropriately pricedand genuinely within your understanding before depositing.Liquidity Provision: Earning from Market StructureAutomated market makers transformed decentralised trading by replacing order bookswith liquidity pools, reservoirs of two assets in fixed ratio that execute swapsalgorithmically. Providing capital to these pools earns a share of trading fees andconstitutes one of the most structurally interesting yield strategies in DeFi. It alsointroduces impermanent loss, the yield mechanism's defining risk that most introductorytreatments significantly underexplain.Impermanent loss occurs when the price ratio between the two assets in a liquidity poolchanges after your deposit. The AMM algorithm automatically rebalances the pool'scomposition in response to trades, effectively selling the appreciating asset and buying thedepreciating one. If ETH doubles in price after you deposit into an ETH-USDC pool, you endup with less ETH and more USDC than if you had simply held the tokens separately. Thedifference between what you actually hold and what you would have held, theimpermanent loss, is a real cost that must be offset by fee income for liquidity provision tobe profitable. It is called impermanent because if prices return to the original ratio, the lossdisappears. In practice, many pools maintain long-term price divergence, making the losspermanent.%u2014 214 %u2014
