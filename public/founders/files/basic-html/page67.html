Its working groups are more technically open than the CBN%u2019s.NFIUNigerian FinancialIntelligence UnitMinistry / Oversight: Office of the National Security Adviser / operationally independentCore mandate: Receiving, analysing, and disseminating financial intelligence;coordinating Nigeria%u2019s AML/CFT framework; overseeing suspicious transaction reporting.Digital assets role: Every VASP operating in Nigeria must register with the NFIU and fileSuspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) and Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs). The NFIUissues AML/CFT compliance guidelines specifically for VASPs and oversees theimplementation of Nigeria%u2019s FATF obligations.Founder watch-out: The NFIU is not optional. It is a separate registration and complianceobligation from your SEC licence. Many founders discover this late, and the NFIU%u2019senforcement posture has hardened significantly since Nigeria%u2019s 2023 grey listing. Buildyour STR and CTR filing infrastructure before you need it.EFCCEconomic and FinancialCrimes CommissionMinistry / Oversight: Office of the Attorney General of the Federation / Ministry ofJusticeCore mandate: Investigation and prosecution of economic and financial crimes, includingmoney laundering, advance fee fraud, and cybercrime.Digital assets role: The EFCC investigates and prosecutes criminal offences involvingdigital assets %u2014 including platforms used to facilitate fraud, money laundering, orunlicensed securities offerings. It has broad investigative powers including accountfreezing, asset seizure, and device confiscation.Founder watch-out: The EFCC is not a licensing authority and is not part of yourday-to-day compliance landscape, until something goes wrong. Operating an unlicensedVASP, facilitating transactions that are later found to involve illicit funds, or failing torespond adequately to an NFIU referral can bring the EFCC to your door. AML complianceis your best protection. You will need SCUML (Special Control Unit Against MoneyLaundering) from EFCC. The registration is mandatory for Designated Non-FinancialBusinesses and Professions (DNFBPs) in Nigeria to comply with anti-money launderinglaws. It is essential for businesses handling high-value cash transactions, including realestate, law firms, VASPs, car dealers, luxury goods dealers, and hotels, to operate legallyand maintain bank accounts.NAICOMNational InsuranceCommissionMinistry / Oversight: Federal Ministry of FinanceCore mandate: Regulation and supervision of the Nigerian insurance industry.Digital assets role: Relevant to digital asset businesses that operate insurance-adjacentproducts, require fidelity bonds (as VASP licensing does), or are building parametricinsurance products on blockchain. NAICOM has participated in fintech roadmapconsultations relevant to digital products in the insurance space.Founder watch-out: Most digital asset founders will not have a primary regulatoryrelationship with NAICOM.
