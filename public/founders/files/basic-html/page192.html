                                    THE FOUNDER'S GUIDE TO BUILDING IN REGULATED MARKETSThe term used in South Africa (and in the EU's MiCA regulation) to describe businesses providing servicesrelated to crypto assets %u2014 equivalent to the VASP designation used in Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, and by FATF.South Africa's Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) began licensing CASPs from June 2023, makingSouth Africa one of the first African countries to operationalise a formal VASP/CASP licensing regime.South Africa (selective coverage): The FSCA is the primary regulator for CASPs in South Africa. Firms providingcrypto asset services were required to apply for CASP authorisation by November 2023. The FinancialIntelligence Centre (FIC) oversees AML/CFT obligations for CASPs under the Financial Intelligence Centre Act.See also: VASP, FSCA, FIC (South Africa), MiCACBN (Central Bank of Nigeria)Nigeria's apex monetary authority, responsible for monetary policy, financial system stability, and theregulation of banks and payment systems under BOFIA 2020. The CBN's relationship with digital assets hasevolved significantly: its 2021 circular restricted banks from servicing crypto transactions, but subsequentpolicy shifts (including the 2023 relaxation for licensed entities) now allow banks to work with SEC-licensedVASPs. The CBN regulates the eNaira (Nigeria's CBDC) and oversees payment service providers.Founders should understand that the CBN and SEC Nigeria have overlapping but distinct jurisdiction over digitalassets: SEC regulates digital assets as securities (investment, trading, custody), while the CBN regulates thepayment rails, monetary aspects, and stablecoin implications. Any product that touches both dimensions %u2014such as a stablecoin payment product %u2014 may require engagement with both regulators.See also: SEC Nigeria, eNaira, BOFIA, Stablecoin, VASPCBDC (Central Bank Digital Currency)A digital form of a country's sovereign currency, issued and backed directly by the central bank. Unlikecryptocurrencies, CBDCs are centralised, government-controlled, and carry the same legal tender status asphysical currency. CBDCs can be retail (accessible to the general public) or wholesale (restricted to financialinstitutions for interbank settlements).Nigeria launched the eNaira %u2014 Africa's first CBDC %u2014 in October 2021, issued by the CBN. The eNaira operateson a permissioned blockchain and is designed to expand financial inclusion and facilitate remittances. Adoptionhas been slower than anticipated, and the CBN has issued several revised guidelines to expand use cases.See also: eNaira, Stablecoin, Digital CurrencyCDD (Customer Due Diligence)The process by which regulated entities %u2014 including VASPs %u2014 verify the identity of their customers,understand the nature and purpose of the customer relationship, and assess the risk of financial crime. CDDis a foundational AML requirement. It encompasses identity verification, source of funds checks, andongoing monitoring. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) applies to higher-risk customers such as PEPs.%u2014 192 %u2014
